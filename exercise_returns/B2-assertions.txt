1: What is the difference between concurrent and immediate assertions?

Immediate Assertions

>Checked instantly when simulation reaches the line.
>Used inside always or initial blocks.
>Checks simple, single-time conditions.
>Good for debugging and basic sanity checks.
>Example: assert (A == B);

Concurrent Assertions

> Checked over time, using clocked behavior.
>Can be used inside or outside procedural blocks.
>Checks sequences (e.g., "A happens, then B after 2 clocks").
>Good for protocol checks, timing, and formal verification.
>Example: assert property (@(posedge clk) A |-> ##2 B);


2: How does validation differ from verification?

Validation: Ensures the product meets the user's needs. It checks if you are building the right product.

Verification: Ensures the product works according to the design specification. It checks if you are building the product correctly.
